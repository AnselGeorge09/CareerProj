Education Requirements for Software Developers/Engineers

Bachelor's Degree

Most common and preferred by employers. Typically takes 4 years to complete.

Most common majors:

  • Computer Science
  • Software Engineering
  • Information Technology
  • Information Systems
  • Computer Information Systems

Associate Degree

Can be a starting point, mostly taking 2 years. May need to transfer to a bachelor's program later.

Master's Degree

Some companies prefer or require this for top positions. Takes an additional 1-2 years after a bachelor's degree. Can lead to higher salaries.

Education for Software Developers

To become a software developer, you typically need to earn a bachelor's degree in computer science or a related field, which takes around 4 years to complete. However, there are alternative paths, such as coding bootcamps, which can last a few months and provide a faster entry into the job market. Professional certificates are also available, offering a quicker alternative to a full degree. Self-study is another option, although it can be challenging. Building personal projects is an excellent way to demonstrate your skills to potential employers and can be included in a resume or portfolio.

Note: While a bachelor's degree is the most common path, other factors like internships, projects, and portfolios are essential regardless of educational background.
